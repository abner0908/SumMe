Matapos ang sanda­ling pananahimik, muli na namang binanatan ni Senadora Miriam Defensor-Santiago ang itinuturing niyang mortal na kaaway sa Mataas na Kapulungan ng Kongreso na si Senate Minority Leader Juan Ponce Enrile, sa pagkakataong ito, ang breakwater sa Port Irene, Cagayan ang napagdiskitahan ng senadora.Sa sulat na ipinadala ni Defensor-Santiago kay Department of Justice (DOJ) Secretary Leila de Lima, hiniling ng mambabatas sa kalihim na imbestigahan din nito ang paggastos ng P5.101 bilyon, limang taon na ang nakararaan, sa pagtatayo ng breakwater sa nabanggit na freeport.Sa sulat kay De Lima ay ibinunyag din ni Defensor-Santiago na iprayoridad sa imbestigasyon ang nagtatagong dating chief of staff ni Enrile na si Atty. Lucila “Gigi” Reyes dahil kumpanya umano ng pamil­ya nito ang nakakopo sa kontrata.“I humbly submit that it would certainly be productive to investigate the use of the names of his chief of staff – Atty. Jessica Lucilla ‘Gigi’ Reyes – and her family in the disbursement of some P5.101 billion in public funds,” pagbubunyag ni Defensor-Santiago.“Enrile was able to insert this humongous amount in the budget for the Cagayan Freeport for the last five years. It appears that Enrile used their names to surreptitiously morph the Freeport into the main vehicle for smuggling and other illicit operations,” pagpapatuloy pa ni Defensor-Santiago.“Port Irene – described as ‘the backbone of the Freeport’ – consumed the amount of P5.101 billion in public funds for the construction of a breakwater. The builder was Sta. Elena Construction, headed by Alice Eduardo as president and CEO,” ayon pa sa mambabatas.
